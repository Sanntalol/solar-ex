SOLAR SYSTEM

Inner Planets

Inner Planet The planets Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, are called terrestrial because they have a compact, rocky surface like Earth's terra firma. The terrestrial planets are the four innermost planets in the solar system.

Mercury

  • The Closest planet to the sun

  • Covered with many craters

Outer Planets

Outer Planet The outer planets are gas giants Jupiter and Saturn and ice giants Uranus and Neptune. Beyond Neptune, a newer class of smaller worlds called dwarf planets reign, including longtime favorite Pluto.

Jupiter The fifth planet from the sun. Jupiter is the biggest planet in the solar system. Jupiter is made up of gases. The big red spot Jupiter is a giant storm.
